Day 3 · Sunday, May 24

Grand Canyon of Yellowstone · Lamar Valley Sunset

Start8:00 AM
Drive~4.5 hrs total
Sunset~8:35 PM
SleepAntler Lodge, Gardiner MT
Today: Grand Canyon of the Yellowstone first (morning from West Yellowstone), then east to Lamar Valley for the long golden-hour wildlife session, then back west through Mammoth to Gardiner and Antler Lodge. Lamar is the trip highlight — wolves and grizzlies peak at dusk. Stay all the way to ~8:35 PM.

Schedule

8:00 – 8:15 AM
Quick bites + coffee
15 min
Three Bear Lodge breakfast buffet — grab a quick plate and coffee. Pack snacks for the Lamar Valley stretch tonight.
~1 hr 15 min east via Madison → Norris → Canyon Village
9:30 – 11:15 AM
Grand Canyon of the Yellowstone
~1.75 hrs · must-do
South Rim first — Artist Point for the iconic canyon + Lower Falls panorama (~30 min, absolutely do not skip). Then North Rim: Lookout Point + Inspiration Point for the different angle down into the canyon (~30 min). The rhyolite canyon walls glow yellow and orange in morning light.
11:30 AM – 12:15 PM
Lunch — Canyon Village
~45 min
Canyon Lodge Cafeteria or Canyon General Store deli. Quick fuel stop before the long drive east to Lamar.
~1 hr east via Tower-Roosevelt Junction → Lamar Valley
1:15 – 8:35 PM
Lamar Valley — afternoon into sunset
~7 hrs · bring binoculars
Drive the full valley to Soda Butte and back. Park at pullouts and scan the hillsides. Bison herds are a given — grizzlies, wolves, and pronghorn peak at golden hour. The Lamar Canyon wolf pack has been active this spring. Stay all the way through sunset ~8:35 PM — this is the trip's single best wildlife window. Don't leave early.
~45 min back west — Lamar → Tower → Mammoth → North Entrance → Gardiner
~9:20 PM
Dinner in Gardiner
~1 hr
Rustler's Roost or Iron Horse Bar & Grill — both walkable from Antler Lodge. Check hours in advance; Gardiner restaurants close early on weeknights.
~10:30 PM
Antler Lodge, Gardiner
overnight
Right at the North Entrance of Yellowstone, 5 miles from Mammoth. Perfect jumping-off point for Mammoth first thing tomorrow before the drive to Bozeman.

Day 4 · Monday, May 25

Mammoth Hot Springs · Fly Home from Bozeman

Start8:00 AM
Depart Gardiner12:30 PM hard cutoff
Flight4:29 PM BZN (Bozeman)
⚠ Flight is 4:29 PM from Bozeman (BZN) — arrive airport by 3:15 PM. Drive from Gardiner to Bozeman is ~1 hr 20 min through Livingston. Leave Gardiner by 12:30 PM sharp. No sit-down lunch — pack snacks the night before and eat on the road north.

Schedule

8:00 – 8:30 AM
Breakfast + pack up
30 min
Grab breakfast at Antler Lodge or a Gardiner coffee spot. Check out and load the car. Short drive south into the park to Mammoth.
~5 min south from Gardiner → Mammoth Hot Springs
8:45 – 10:30 AM
Mammoth Hot Springs Terraces
~1.75 hrs · last big wow
Lower Terraces boardwalk first — Palette Spring, Liberty Cap, and the active travertine formations (~45 min). Then Upper Terrace Drive loop for the elevated overlooks and quieter pools (~45 min). Morning light makes the orange-white terraces glow. Elk are often grazing right on the boardwalk.
10:30 – 11:15 AM
Lunch to-go — Mammoth Terrace Grill
~45 min
Pick up boxed lunches or wraps from the Terrace Grill quick-service window. Eat in the car heading north — the Paradise Valley drive along the Yellowstone River is stunning anyway.
~5 min north — Mammoth → Gardiner (North Entrance)
12:30 PM
Depart Gardiner — hard cutoff
do not delay
Through the Roosevelt Arch and north on US-89 through Paradise Valley. Yellowstone River views the whole way. Livingston is your gas stop ~45 min in. Beautiful final drive through Montana ranch country.
~1 hr 20 min north — Gardiner → Livingston → Bozeman (BZN)
1:50 – 2:10 PM
Bozeman Yellowstone International Airport (BZN)
return rental + check in
Return rental car and check in. ~2 hr buffer before 4:29 PM flight. BZN has a great terminal with mountain views and good food options. Spend a few minutes in Livingston for gas and a stretch en route — it's a classic Montana railroad town.
